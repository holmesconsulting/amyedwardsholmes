Thought Leadership

Leading Transformational Data & AI Strategies

Leaders at all levels are struggling with one challenge today – how to embrace the benefits of AI and realize the value for my organization. Are you wondering…

  • What are the best applications for AI in my sector?
  • Do you have a vision for the use of AI?
  • Is my data AI ready?
  • Is your workforce equipped to leverage AI?
  • Do you have a roadmap towards AI adoption?
  • What are the ethical and regulatory considerations for the adoption of AI?
  • How do I include stakeholders and customer input into my data and AI strategy?
For over two decades, Amy Edwards Holmes has helped leaders across sectors answer these vital questions and develop complex strategies to embrace emerging technology. Amy specializes in developing complex data and AI strategies that focus on five core principles:
  • Value proposition - Simple problem framing that creates urgency
  • Stakeholder mapping and engagement - Building allies for shared goals
  • Leadership alignment - Decision-makers to align on a vision
  • Workforce engagement - Develop solutions with internal stakeholders
  • Incremental technical development - Flexibility to test with users and build agile solutions
Amy helps organizations develop successful data and AI strategies that overcome the common barriers to change in complex bureaucracies. Amy has successfully built cultures of continuous improvement that lead to organization-wide transformations.

Advisory Services

  • Executive Vision for Data & AI Transformation: Define the desired future state for the organization and align key stakeholders around the vision.
  • Assessment of Current Data & AI Readiness: Assessing the existing data strategy and workforce capacity through document reviews, surveys, and interviews to determine the data maturity level across five key components.
  • Data & AI Strategy Development: Develop a multi-year actionable data & AI strategy that includes tactics, activities, goals and key milestones that align to the organizational mission.
  • Data & AI Strategy Implementation: Advisory support for Chief Data Officers and other executive leadership to achieve results and optimize and transform mission delivery.
  • Evaluation & Planning: Review the progress of the Data & AI Strategy to support planning for the future with a commitment to continuous improvement.

About Amy

Amy is currently the Executive Director of the Bloomberg Center for Government Excellence at Johns Hopkins University, where she led efforts to empower public sector organizations across more than 37 countries to enhance data capacity, leverage AI, and improve public services. Her leadership has driven the development of innovative platforms like City AI Connect, which was recognized by Fast Company in 2024 for fostering responsible AI use in government.
Prior to joining Johns Hopkins, Amy held key leadership positions at the U.S. Department of the Treasury, and as the Deputy Assistant Secretary, she oversaw the financial operations for the entire federal government and led initiatives to enhance data transparency, including the policy, passage and implementation of the first open data law in the U.S. the Digital Accountability and Transparency Act (DATA Act). Her work established an improved USAspending.gov, the first government-wide digital product built using agile development principles. Amy's extensive experience in government performance also includes serving as the Director of the Task Force on Government Performance for the U.S. Senate Budget Committee, where she advised on technology and data policies, and led the passage of key bipartisan legislation.
Earlier in her career, Amy directed performance management programs at The Pew Charitable Trusts and the Council for Excellence in Government, where she supported state and local governments in adopting innovative practices. She has also served as an advisor to governors, state officials, and Congressional leaders on improving government efficiency and transparency.
Amy is a Fellow of the National Academy of Public Administration and has served as a U.S. delegate to international forums such as the United Nations and OECD. She is also a Member of the U.S. Open Government Federal a Committee where she offers advice on making the government more open and transparent to strengthen democracy. She holds a Master of Public Administration from Troy University and a Bachelor of Science from Radford University. >With a proven record of advancing data-driven governance, Amy is a thought leader in public sector innovation, dedicated to leveraging emerging technologies for the betterment of government operations and public services.

Amy's Mission:

Use data to design public services that meet the needs of the community. Starting on the frontlines early in her career Amy recognized that public programs were not informed with residents' perspectives and lived experience. For 25 years, Amy has worked to reform policy design to put people at the center of solutions to enable people centered policies.
